About

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science at Columbia University, where I lead the Verifiable Learning Group. My research sits at the intersection of machine learning, security, and cryptography, with the goal of building AI systems whose behavior can be independently verified rather than merely trusted.

Before joining Columbia in 2025, I completed my Ph.D. at the University of California, Berkeley, and was a postdoctoral researcher at MIT CSAIL. My work has been recognized with an NSF CAREER Award and a Best Paper Award at NeurIPS. I care deeply about broadening participation in computing and mentor students from historically underrepresented groups in CS.

Prospective students: I am recruiting Ph.D. students for Fall 2027. If you are interested in verifiable ML, trustworthy AI, or the systems that make them practical, please reach out and mention this note in your email.
