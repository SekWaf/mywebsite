About

§

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science at Columbia University, where I lead the Physical Intelligence Lab. My research develops machine learning for physical simulation and reinforcement learning — building differentiable simulators and agents that learn to model, predict, and control complex physical systems.

I am also drawn to the physics of antimatter, where I develop machine-learned models of matter–antimatter interactions. Before joining Columbia in 2025, I completed my PhD at UC Berkeley and a postdoc at MIT CSAIL. My work has been recognized with an NSF CAREER Award and a Best Paper Award at NeurIPS.

I am recruiting PhD students for Fall 2027.
